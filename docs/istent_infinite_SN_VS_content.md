## Evidence anchors

- **Pivotal trial** — per PubMed: Sarkisian et al., *J Glaucoma* 2023; PMID 36260288; [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1097/IJG.0000000000002141). Standalone iStent infinite (3 stents) in OAG uncontrolled by prior surgery or maximum tolerated medical therapy. Prospective, multicentre, single-arm, open-label; 72 eyes; 12 months.
  - **Responders:** ~76% (≥20% IOP reduction at M12, same/fewer meds)
  - **Mean diurnal IOP reduction at M12:** **5.9 mmHg** (from medicated baseline ~23.4 mmHg)
  - **Deeper response:** 53% achieved ≥30% reduction
  - **Safety:** no explants, infection, device-related interventions, or hypotony
- **Indication** — adult POAG in whom prior medical *and* surgical treatment has failed; **standalone** use. Maps to **L5 surgical-eligible** in the glaucoma funnel.
- **Platform** — iStent trabecular micro-bypass franchise: ~20-year legacy, 300+ publications, 3M+ devices (Glaukos, company-reported).

> **Anchor discipline:** all efficacy/safety claims trace to the pivotal trial (5.9 mmHg). Platform-legacy and mechanism claims are Glaukos company-reported and flagged as such.

## SCIENTIFIC NARRATIVE — 14 statements, 4 pillars

*EvHub-S two-statement banner structure: each pillar opens with a **lead** statement, remaining statements are **support**.*

### Pillar 1 — Disease & Unmet Need
- **S1 (lead)** — A defined OAG population stays uncontrolled despite maximum tolerated medical therapy and prior glaucoma surgery.
- **S2** — For these patients, the corridor between failed therapy and invasive filtration/tube surgery has historically been narrow.
- **S3** — Persistent elevated IOP drives ongoing, irreversible optic nerve damage — durable IOP control is the central goal.

### Pillar 2 — Mechanism & Innovation
- **S4 (lead)** — iStent infinite restores physiologic aqueous outflow via three trabecular micro-bypass stents, implanted standalone.
- **S5** — Stents create multiple arcs of outflow across Schlemm's canal while occupying a minimal fraction of it, preserving natural anatomy.
- **S6** — First standalone micro-invasive implantable option in its indication — extends interventional glaucoma to later-stage, refractory disease.

### Pillar 3 — Clinical Efficacy
- **S7 (lead)** — ~76% of a failed-prior-therapy OAG population met the responder endpoint at 12 months.
- **S8** — Mean diurnal IOP fell **5.9 mmHg** from a medicated baseline of ~23.4 mmHg at M12.
- **S9** — Responses achieved on the same or fewer IOP-lowering medication classes.
- **S10** — 53% achieved ≥30% IOP reduction without additional surgical intervention.

### Pillar 4 — Safety & Procedural Profile
- **S11 (lead)** — Favourable safety: no explants, infection, device-related interventions, or hypotony in the pivotal trial.
- **S12** — Micro-invasive, angle-based approach minimises tissue disruption vs filtration/tube surgery.
- **S13** — Standalone implantation preserves future surgical options — supports a stepwise interventional pathway.
- **S14** — Backed by a two-decade iStent evidence legacy across the trabecular micro-bypass platform.
